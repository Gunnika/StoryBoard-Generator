Beowulf: An Anglo-Saxon Epic Poem is considered the oldest surviving old English poem,over three thousand alliterative verses celebrating the Great-Dane who protected King Hrothgar, conquered Grendeland was ultimately defeated by a dragon. Though its authorship is unknown, the poem was written between 925 and1025, preserved as the Nowell Codex, badly damaged in a London fire which housed medieval works collected by SirRobert Bruce Cotton, moved to the British Library where it remains today.
